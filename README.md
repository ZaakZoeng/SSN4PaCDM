# SSN4PaCDM
Constructing sample-specific networks (SSN) for different sample types to analyze pancreatic cancer (PaC)-associated diabetes mellitus (PaCDM)

Understanding the pathogenesis of pancreatic cancer (PaC)-associated diabetes mellitus (PaCDM) is pivotal for early detection of PaC in individuals with new-onset diabetes. However, the full comprehension of the driver genes contributing to PaCDM pathogenesis remains elusive, primarily due to the limitations of traditional bioinformatics analyses. In this study, we innovatively applied sample-specific network theory, a novel mathematical method, to construct a comprehensive functional gene network. Through meticulous statistical analysis, we identified some candidate genes as highly plausible driver genes. The overexpression of candidate genes in PaC intensified paraneoplastic beta cell dysfunction, triggering endoplasmic reticulum stress and culminating in glucose intolerance in vivo. Our results strongly suggest that candidate genes may indeed serve as key driver genes in the pathogenesis of PaCDM. Detectable molecules downstream of candidate genes hold promise as potential biomarkers for PaCDM screening, thereby facilitating the advancement of early PaC detection strategies.
